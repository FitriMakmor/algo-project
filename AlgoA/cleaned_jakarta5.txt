Indonesian stocks hit yet another circuit breaker Thursday morning, prompting Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX) halt trading 30 minutes fourth time week.

The main gauge, Jakarta Composite Index (JCI), nosedived 5.01 percent mere 37 minutes opened 9 a.m. Jakarta 4,113.65, level unseen since August 2012.

Trading IDX officially halted 9.37 a.m. reopen 10.07 a.m., IDX trading director Laksono Widodo told press Thursday, adding transactions market closed totaled Rp 1.22 trillion (US$75 million).

Foreign investors dumped Rp 461 billion stocks bought Thursday morning.
